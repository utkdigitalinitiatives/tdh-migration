August 19th Apl April 1817 Colo Colonel R.J. Return Jonathan Meigs Dear Sir I had contemplated the pleasure of seeing you during the present summer - but as I am not certain that it will be convenient to take a tour to the westward - I avail myself of all opportunity , by my friend Mr Goodriel , by who I beg leave to remark, any remittance you may be disposed to make me, will no doubt be safely delivered. I understood that in the cause of business public & private, you have of late years been blessed with much prosperity - in this I sincerely rejoice as I could not fail to do, at any increase of the wealth of one who from my earliest days I have been accustomed to respect - and it is a gratification to me to know, that you have, the friend of my former days, now feels a position honorable to himself, & important to his country. Sir your favor of 20th Feb February 1812 , you

contemplate an annual payment to me of 175 dollars, till until the account between us should be closed - as the proposed arrangement service through mr pope was afterwards altered, & as it has so happened that no payment has Since been made me, should it be convenient to send me an amount equal, to what an annual payment of that amount to this time could be - it would greatly serve an accommodate me - it would be about four times 175 dolls dollars As probably however it may be perfectly convenient to you to settle the whole I have given Mr Goodriel a copy of your statement to me, of which I suffice you have the counterpart . In your former of 20th Feb 1812 you mention that you had sent your son two hundred dollars on my account - this was perfectly satisfactory, and is the Same I presume mentioned in your statement, & which at that time left a balance due me, as you Stated of Eleven hundred & twenty six dollars 33 cents on this sum the interest will be calculated to the present time, and on

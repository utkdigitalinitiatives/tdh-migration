Alexa Alexandria La Louisiana 7th March 1850 Dear Col Colonel On my return from the city about the middle of Feby February I found your long & very welcome letter of the 6th Jan.y January and was gratified to hear that all were well. I remained in the city a month, two weeks of the time on my back very ill, many of my friends thought the jig was up, but it was another failure & I am now as stout & as healthy as ever, the fact is, I am kept so cornfounded busy that I have not time to think of being sick or dying within. My last years expenses has almost ruined me, so you see that a man has molly to break before he is prepared for sugar, they were very little short of Twenty Thousand dollars, and not yet near fixed as I wish to be & hope to be if I live. I red received a letter from Sarah expressing a wish to sour three of the negroes that are to be sold, & wants my aid, my opinion in regard to the matter coincides with yours (vig) as they are to be sold under excention they will sell high & for mor more than this value, I shall therefore advise Sarah is such should be the case to let them go, if the oldest should sell for no mor than what she should be worht to her, & you or Butler will bug her for her & wifoverur of it, I will remit the amt to you immediately through Hill McLean Hes. but it is nonsense for one not able, to pay a high price for a negro be-

I have been expecting Tom down for some time Dr Doctor Butlers formerly have been with us for six weeks & will remain with us until his return from Baton Rouge he bring in the senate from Natchetchez We are all in good health, Erin is off at school & well. Give our best love to Cousin Patsy & family, love & family, Aunt Butler, & all our friends, & accept my sincere progress for the good health of all & your cousin to Democracy Your Friend & relative Tho Thomas .

John Sevier Governor in and over the State of Tennessee To all to whom these presents shall come Whereas at a Court began and held for the District of Mero at Nashville before the Honorable David Campbell , Hugh S White and John Overton esquires Judges of our Superior Courts of law and equity on the Second monday of May in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and five, and of the Independence of the United States the twenty ninth, Samuel Forester was arraigned tried and found guilty of murdering a certain female negro Slave named Mille, the property of a certain Garrett Fitzgerald, for which murder the said Samuel Forrester was Sentenced to be punished with death, and the Sentence to be put into execution by the Sheriff of Davidson County on the fifteenth day of June the present month. And whereas on the fourth day of June Instant, I granted a respite of the execution of the said Samuel Forrester until

until the eighteenth day of August next:__ Now know ye that I the said John Sevier Governor &c and et cetera , by virtue of the power and authority in one vested do grant a further respite and prolongations of the punishment and execution of the said Samuel Forrester and do by these presents respite and prolong the said punishment and execution of him the said Samuel Forrester until the Twentieth day of august next, and until between the hours of Twelve in the forenoon and four in the afternoon of the same day, at which time, to wit, on the Twentieth day of august next, between the hours of twelve in the forenoon and four in the afternoon of the same day, execution of the Judgement of the judges of the Superior Courts of law and equity in and for the said State of Tennessee, be made and done upon him the said Samuel Forrester by the Sheriff of Davidson County at the same gallows and place assigned and decreed for that purpose for those purpose by the judges aforesaid, to wit, the public gallows of Davidson County __ In Testimony whereof I have hereunto set my hand and

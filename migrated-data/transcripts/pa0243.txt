State of Tennessee To the sheriff of Rhea County Greetings. We command you to take William Cochrine if to be found in your county and him safly safely keep so as to hear him at the next court of pleas and quarter sessions to be held for the county of Rhea and in the town of Washington at the courthouse on the first Monday of August next to answer Joseph Tompson of a plea of Trespas Trespass on the case to his damages five hundred Dollars and have you then there this Writ ――― Witness Daniel Rollings Clerk of said county court at office in Washington the first monday of may 1820 & 44 year of our Independence ― D. Rollings , Clerk ――――― Know all men by this presents that we Joseph Tompson and James Prentice are Bound to William Cochrine in the sum of $250. to be paid of the said Joseph Tompson shall with effect prosecute a suit by him this day commenced against the said William Cochrine or in case of falure failure pay all costs of this suit Witness our hands and seals the 27th of July 1820. Joseph Thompson hand drawn seal James Prentice hand drawn seal on the back of the above casses cases & bond The Endorsement, follows was as follows Case & respondent Joseph Thompson vs William Cochrine signed 27 July 1820 Came to hand the same day signed Wm William Francis Sheriff not to be found in my county Wm Francis }The above casses and bond is the first that appears and should therefore preced preceed the casses on the foregoing page R. State of Tennessee Rhea County } February session 1821 Joseph Tompson by his atorney attorney comp complains of William Cocrine in the custody of the sheriff of a plea of trespass on the case for that where as the said Joseph Tompson now is a good true honest great and faithful citesin citizen and as such hath allways likeined and shall continue to behave conducted himself and untill until the commiting committing of the said grievances by the said William Cocrine as hereinafter mentioned was always respected esteemed and acredited by and among his all his neighbours and other good and worth citizens to whom he was in any way known to be a person of good name fame and credit at to wit in the county afforesaid aforesaid and whereas also the said Joseph Thompson hath not ever been guilty as untill until the time of the commiting of the of the said several grievances by the said William Cochrine as hereinafter mentioned been suspected to have been guilty of larciny larceny or any other such crimes by means of which said premises he the said Joseph Tompson before the commiting of the said grevances grievances by the William Cochrine as hereinafter mentioned had universaly universally obtained the good opinion and credit of all his neighbours and other good and worthy citizens to whom he was in any way known at to wit in the county aforesaid Yet the said William well knowing the premises but greatly injuring the happy state and condition of the said Joseph and contriving and wickedly and maliciously intending to injure the said Joseph in his said good name fame and credit and to bring him into public scandal infamy and disgrace with and among all his neighbours and other good and worthy citizens and to cause it to be suspected and believed by more neighbours and citizens that he the said Joseph had been guilty and was guilty of larceny

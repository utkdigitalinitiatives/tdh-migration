Nashville Sunday Morning April 14th 1833 To Mrs T. B. Hamilton Rock Cave My Dear Mother— I know that there is but few things that affords you so much pleasure as to hear from your children and for this reason I now take ⁁ my pen to write you a few lines— Brother and myself have enjoyed perfect health since I wrote to you last and hope that these lines will find you enjoying the same blessing I received a letter from John O and L S Gilbert a week or two ago I was glad to hear that they were enjoying good health and that they still expressed a warm regard of friendship for me.— They stated that you had received my letter I wrote to them about the murder that had been committed in this town and by referring to that you will see a detail of the crime the murderer is in jail and is to have his trial in May next. The father of the young man that got killed was a gambler. When the father is of a dissipated character the son is apt to follow the example let led before him. Had the father been an upright and correct man and had let led ⁁ a good example before son it is very probable that he would not have been killed by a merciless assassin. He was a young man just in the prime of life and had left his home to visit West Point and perhaps he expected some future period to be some

great man — But alas just as the bud hath put forth its flower it is withered by the schorching scorching sun I feel thankful that my father let led a good example before his children and for so doing the Lord blessed him about five years ago he entered in to the joy of his Lord and is now singing around the throne of his redeemer I think I sustain some of the principles which he had and which carried him through this world with such respect— But the Christian principles which he passed and which bore him through the dark valley and shadow of death and which landed him safe in the port of heaven I do not possess But I am seeking for it and by the assisting grace of God I am determined to have it The assurance that I have of his being there and my little brother and my Christian relations Also ought not this to teach me to persevere to try and meet them yes Mother it does and I feel resolved never to give up the struggle untill until I shall come that conquerer conqueror Oh may the Lord help me on and enable me by faith to believe on him and obtain this blessing Oh Mother pray for me that I may be delivered from the bondage of sin shortly— Tell brother James that he must W Write to me shortly Give my love to him kiss brother Joseph and tell him

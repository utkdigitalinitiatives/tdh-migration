Chickasaw Nation april 22d 1808 Sir a Native of this Nation came to me this morning requesting me to write to you for information respecting his Brother who he was informed was killed (perhaps shot) in your Neighbourhood Neighborhood some time during the last fall or winter. he appears anctious anxious to know the truth of the report. The Chickasaw that is missing has been absent for two or three years in which time he has been to Washington City & perhaps to Baltimore and Philadelphia ; last year had returned home ward as far as Duck river , there exchanged some property he had obtained by some means for whiskey and returned back-shortly after which it is reported he was killed in, or in the vicinity of the Neigh

bourhood in which you reside. should any such circumstance have happened, it probably would have come to your knowledge, and in order to establish the confidence of the Nation of Indians among whom I reside, in the Government of the U. United States ; I am willing to give them every explanitory explanatory satisfaction in my power; where a doubt could subsist of their confidence being misplaced. I am the more particular as some of the neighbouring Indians (the Creek s in particular) appear to be so ignorant of their own welfare, as to commit depredations on our frontier settlements. will you be so obliging as too inform me by letter whether any such circumstance (as the above named) has happened within your neighborhood, and if in the afirmative affirmative please name the circumstances. I am respectfully sir your Ob Servt Obedient Servant Thomas Wright U. S. Agt States Agent to the Chickasaws Gen General __ Winchester

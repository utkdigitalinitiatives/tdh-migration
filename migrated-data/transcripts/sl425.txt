Carthage Tennessee 7th October 1844 Dear Sir I hope it will not be un acceptable to you to hear from your well tried friends in Smith old Smith I am Sure there can be nonpareil of your friends therein more Tennessee so devoted to your elevation to the office of President of this Great Nation than that portion comprising the Democracy of Smith They have been long and well tried we are here then battleing battling away like men not at all faulting or hesitating our position has been nobly maintained; and although opposed by greater numbers our confidence is growing stronger. we have made repeated charges on their whole line with the means in our power and I am happy to inform you the result has told in our favour. I am convinced the the the excitement is now changed to this part of the State and is on the increase daily. We are still organizing military companies. No exertions or sacrifices are now spared on the part of our Phalaxy Democratic Phalanx; while all the other on the other side are opposed to us a most formidable enemy who Seeks every advantage that fraude fraud or falsehood can invent we have certainly arrived at a most powerful crises in the History of our country on the one hand we See in your elevation to the office of chief magistrate a resteration restoration of all those first Principles dear to freemen that we have enjoyed under the Patriots washington Jefferson and Jackson while on the other hand should defeat be our fate (which God forbid the long train of evils and measures to be fastened ere us and our children our childrens children are too Horid to mention I am Pleased to hear from various parts of the state the most whatever other pats parts of

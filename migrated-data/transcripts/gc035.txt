Rural Rest 18th May 1838 . My Dear Son Before this time you have reached Calhone , the plaice place of rendezvous, and by this I presume you have learnt a little of the reedements rudiments of a soaldier soldier in camps; therefore if I give you a short treatice treatise on the duties, acts, and performaces performances of a soaldier, it will be more perseptible perceptible to your understanding than would have been on any former occasion. It is expected your officers will instruct you on all the various duties of a soaldier in camp; also, the rules & regulations, by law that are necessarily established, to keep good order and disciplin discipline in the army. They will also inform you of the pennilities penalties and punnishmints punishments annexed by violating and disobaying disobeying such laws and regulation. Whilst learning what the various duties of a soaldier are; you should bear in mind, the soaldier that prides in his duty, will be always ready and chearfully cheerfully perform it, whilst health permits,. therefore in I incourage encourage you, by the love of duty to purform perform the part of a valient valiant Soaldier soldier . In treating on the love of duty I would not have you to think, you are to be at every bodies beck and call, and to do any thing that might call on you to do, no; that would show two much good natured ignorence ignorance ; I would have you to inspire confidence & stability in your self, and courage to suffer no imposition in any way. I have been an old soaldier, and have seen, and know, many imprudent acts and deeds by soaldiers soldiers in the army; such as many have recieved received severe punishment, others, with their carracters characters so defaimed defamed that they bare Since I come in town I understand you are have all arrived at Calhone saft safe , and are all well, In presenting my respects to Capt Captain Yoakum tell him if I would be pleased he would write to me- JaMKing

If any others wish interested to perusing it let them do so. I wish you William & Albert assist each other as occasion requires, and act generously & fealingly feelingly towards each other-

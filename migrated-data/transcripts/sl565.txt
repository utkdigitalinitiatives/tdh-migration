Alabama Bluff Feby February 21st 1815 Genl General Winchester Sir, The alarming appearance of a party of hostile Indians on our frontiers, and the weak & unprotected State, not only of the adjoining County, East of Tombigbee , but especially of the settlements on this River, induces me to State to your excelency excellency and suggest the propriety of making some adequate provision to secure the Citizens against their attacks___ I beg you to be assured that while I am perfectly satisfied of your attention to every part of the District over which you command I State this circumstance only on the recollection that you have more important Sections of the country to protect, but at the same time Knowing it to be perfectly agreeable to your feelings to extend that protection as far as possable possible , and equally desirable to know where it may be wanting ____ permit me to suggest father farther , that your permission of the return of the Citizens drafted from this Settlement would give the country, perhaps the most efficient force that could be provided, as they have every thing to protect, and father add the pleasing consideration, that they can be providing bread for which the country will inevitably suffer ere long___ You will no doubt here learn that the Indians have

plundered & fired on the Citizens, and evinced every determination again to second their British Allies.____ I take the liberty to Suggest heads of families, persons extreamly extremely necessary to their Settlements residing on the frontiers, whose return would be peculiarly desirable_ Geo Morgan __ William Hays __ Richd Odem _ and Caleb Monterief __ David Simmons , Rubin Pearson James Ford Stephens Williams Should the above Citizens be returned we Shall be, we hope, perfectly Safe__ I beg you to be assured of the high Sentiments of esteem with which I am most respectfully Your Excelencys Mst Obt Servt Most Obedient Servant George Fisher His Excelency Genl Winchester P.S. Post Script Should you think proper to release the above mentioned men, & let them be commanded by some person whom you may think adequate to the task_ They would no doubt be of great Service in assisting to destroy the Indians now lurking in the settlements. even should you think proper to order them back____

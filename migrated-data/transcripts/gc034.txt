Alexa Alexandria 30th April 1837 Dear Cousin I again address you & hope this will find you all in the enjoyment of that inestimable blessing & good health I wrote Sarah some weeks since but have had no answer yet, I have no news more than what is common I presume throughout the union (viz, The unprecedented distress in the monied moneyed affars affaires of the country, your Country of course from the failure of one of your banks and the banking house of yeatman & Woods must shun in the calamity, a great number of failures have occured occurred in N. New Orleans , such times have never been seen all confidence last, the commercial community seems to be completely paralyzed nothing doing, merchants will not even pay frieght freight on Cotton to have it to sell, among those who have failed are Thos Thomas . Barrett & my former merchants, then Hermann Briggs &. Co. when I went after the failure of the other house, the latter house failed for five millions, thus came S & L Dick for fifteen million. Burke Wall & Co. amount not yet ascertained, Staunton Buckuer & Co., Leigh & Maddox all larger houses & a great many others not recollected & too numerous to mention, I am told the Mississippi planters are going daily, our planters will hold on particularly on red river

there are not more than two or three in our parish that are much distressed but money is remarkably scarce in consequence of the timidity Banks to discount, we all hope for better times. We have had an unusual dry winter & spring not rain enough to bring up cotton I never have had so bad a stand & prospects so far bad for a cotton crop as my cotton Cant Can't Come up we have not had such a spring for thirty years. Every thing is falling rapidly except pork though that will soon fall Cotton is at this time worth from 6 to 10 cents I was fortunate enough to get my whole crop off at 16 cts cents sound & that very day it commenced falling â€” negroes are selling men at 800 & 850 women in proportion.
